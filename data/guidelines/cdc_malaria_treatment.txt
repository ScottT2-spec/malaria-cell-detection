# CDC Malaria Treatment Guidelines (Summary)
# Source: CDC — Treatment of Malaria: Guidelines for Clinicians (United States)

## Clinical Presentation

### Symptoms
Malaria should be suspected in any patient with fever who has traveled to or lives in a malaria-endemic area. Common symptoms include:
- Fever (may be cyclic — every 48h for P. vivax/ovale, every 72h for P. malariae)
- Chills and rigors
- Headache
- Myalgia (muscle pain)
- Fatigue and malaise
- Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea
- Anemia
- Jaundice (in severe cases)

### Incubation Period
- P. falciparum: 7-14 days
- P. vivax: 12-18 days (can be months with dormant forms)
- P. ovale: 12-18 days (can relapse)
- P. malariae: 18-40 days
- P. knowlesi: 9-12 days

## Species Identification

### P. falciparum — Most Dangerous
- Multiple ring forms in single RBC
- Banana-shaped gametocytes (crescent/sausage shape)
- High parasitemia possible (>5%)
- Can affect RBCs of all ages
- Causes sequestration in capillaries → organ damage

### P. vivax
- Enlarged, pale RBCs with Schüffner's dots
- Amoeboid trophozoites
- Usually <2% parasitemia
- Affects reticulocytes (young RBCs)

### P. ovale
- Oval-shaped infected RBCs with fimbriated edges
- Schüffner's dots present
- Low parasitemia

### P. malariae
- Band-form trophozoites
- Normal-sized RBCs
- Very low parasitemia
- "Rosette" schizonts (daisy-head pattern)

## Treatment Protocols (CDC)

### Uncomplicated P. falciparum (Chloroquine-resistant areas — most of the world)
Option 1: Atovaquone-proguanil (Malarone)
- Adults: 4 tablets daily for 3 days (each tablet: 250mg atovaquone/100mg proguanil)
- Take with food or milk

Option 2: Artemether-lumefantrine (Coartem)
- Adults: 4 tablets per dose at 0, 8, 24, 36, 48, 60 hours (total: 24 tablets)
- Take with fatty food

Option 3: Quinine sulfate + one of: doxycycline, tetracycline, or clindamycin
- Quinine: 542mg base (=650mg salt) every 8h for 3-7 days
- Plus doxycycline 100mg BID for 7 days

### Uncomplicated P. vivax, P. ovale (Chloroquine-sensitive)
- Chloroquine phosphate: 600mg base (=1000mg salt) immediately, then 300mg at 6, 24, 48 hours
- PLUS Primaquine: 30mg base daily for 14 days (anti-relapse therapy)
- Must test G6PD before primaquine!

### Severe Malaria (Any Species)
- IV artesunate: Available through CDC (call CDC Malaria Hotline)
- Dosing: 2.4 mg/kg IV at 0, 12, 24 hours, then daily
- If artesunate unavailable: IV quinidine gluconate with cardiac monitoring
- This is a MEDICAL EMERGENCY — ICU-level care required

## When to Suspect Severe Malaria
- Altered mental status
- Seizures
- Severe anemia (Hb <7)
- Acute kidney injury
- ARDS / pulmonary edema
- DIC / abnormal bleeding
- Shock
- Acidosis
- Parasitemia >5%
- Hypoglycemia

## CDC Malaria Hotline
- During business hours: 770-488-7788
- After hours/weekends/holidays: 770-488-7100 (emergency operations center)
- For IV artesunate availability and clinical consultation

## Follow-up After Treatment
- Repeat blood smears at 48-72 hours to confirm parasite clearance
- If parasitemia not clearing, consider drug resistance or wrong species
- Monitor for 28 days post-treatment
- For P. vivax/ovale: monitor for relapse for up to 1 year
