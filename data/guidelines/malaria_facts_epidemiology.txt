# Malaria Facts & Epidemiology
# Sources: WHO World Malaria Report 2023, WHO Fact Sheet

## Global Burden (2022 Data)
- 249 million malaria cases globally
- 608,000 malaria deaths
- 94% of cases and 95% of deaths occur in Africa
- Children under 5 account for ~80% of all malaria deaths in Africa
- 29 countries account for 95% of global malaria cases

## Most Affected Countries
1. Nigeria: ~27% of global cases
2. Democratic Republic of Congo: ~12%
3. Uganda: ~5%
4. Mozambique: ~4%
5. Ghana: ~3%

## Transmission
- Transmitted through bites of infected female Anopheles mosquitoes
- Peak biting time: dusk to dawn
- Transmission depends on: climate, mosquito species, human immunity, parasite species
- Transmission season varies by region — often aligned with rainy season

## Malaria in Ghana
- Malaria is the leading cause of morbidity in Ghana
- Accounts for ~30% of all outpatient visits
- Ghana Health Service follows WHO treatment guidelines
- ACTs are first-line treatment
- Free malaria treatment for children under 5 and pregnant women through NHIS

## At-Risk Populations
- Children under 5 years (incomplete immunity)
- Pregnant women (reduced immunity during pregnancy)
- People living with HIV/AIDS
- Non-immune migrants, travelers, displaced populations
- Communities in high-transmission areas with limited healthcare access

## Malaria and AI — The Opportunity
- Microscopy-based diagnosis requires trained technicians — scarce in rural areas
- AI-assisted microscopy can help community health workers screen blood samples
- Rapid identification enables faster treatment initiation
- WHO supports digital health interventions for malaria-endemic countries
- Key challenge: maintaining accuracy across diverse sample preparation methods

## Economic Impact
- Malaria costs Africa ~$12 billion annually in lost productivity
- A child with malaria misses ~11 school days
- Families in endemic areas spend up to 25% of income on malaria-related expenses
- Malaria reduction strongly correlates with economic growth

## Drug Resistance Concerns
- Artemisinin partial resistance detected in parts of Southeast Asia and emerging in Africa (Rwanda, Uganda, Eritrea)
- WHO monitoring through Therapeutic Efficacy Studies (TES)
- Partner drug resistance also a concern
- No currently approved alternative to ACTs — protecting their efficacy is critical
