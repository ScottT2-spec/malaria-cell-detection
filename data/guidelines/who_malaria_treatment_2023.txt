# WHO Guidelines for Malaria Treatment (2023 Summary)
# Source: WHO Guidelines for Malaria, 14 March 2023 (WHO/UCN/GMP/2023.01 Rev.1)

## Uncomplicated P. falciparum Malaria

### First-line Treatment
Artemisinin-based combination therapy (ACT) is recommended for uncomplicated P. falciparum malaria.

Recommended ACTs:
- Artemether-lumefantrine (AL): 5-24 mg/kg artemether + 29-144 mg/kg lumefantrine over 3 days
- Artesunate-amodiaquine (AS+AQ): 4 mg/kg/day artesunate + 10 mg/kg/day amodiaquine for 3 days
- Artesunate-mefloquine (AS+MQ): 4 mg/kg/day artesunate for 3 days + 8.3 mg/kg/day mefloquine for 3 days
- Dihydroartemisinin-piperaquine (DHA+PPQ): 4 mg/kg/day DHA + 18 mg/kg/day PPQ for 3 days
- Artesunate-sulfadoxine-pyrimethamine (AS+SP): where SP remains effective

### Treatment Failure
If treatment failure occurs within 14 days, use an alternative first-line ACT (not the same one used initially).
If failure occurs after 14 days, retreat with the same first-line ACT or an alternative ACT.

## Severe P. falciparum Malaria

### Emergency Treatment
Intravenous (IV) or intramuscular (IM) artesunate is the treatment of choice for severe malaria in all age groups.
- Adults and children ≥20 kg: Artesunate 2.4 mg/kg IV or IM at 0, 12, 24 hours, then every 24 hours
- Children <20 kg: Artesunate 3.0 mg/kg per dose (same schedule)
- Minimum 3 doses; continue until patient can take oral medication
- Follow with full 3-day course of ACT

### Pre-referral Treatment
If IV artesunate is not available:
- Intramuscular artesunate, OR
- Rectal artesunate (children <6 years: 10 mg/kg single dose), then refer immediately

### Danger Signs (Severe Malaria Indicators)
- Impaired consciousness or coma
- Prostration (inability to sit/stand/walk)
- Multiple convulsions (>2 in 24 hours)
- Respiratory distress (acidotic breathing)
- Circulatory collapse/shock
- Clinical jaundice plus evidence of other organ dysfunction
- Abnormal spontaneous bleeding
- Pulmonary edema (radiological)

### Laboratory Indicators of Severity
- Hypoglycemia (blood glucose <2.2 mmol/L or <40 mg/dL)
- Metabolic acidosis (plasma bicarbonate <15 mmol/L)
- Severe anemia (Hb <5 g/dL or Hct <15% in children; Hb <7 g/dL in adults)
- Hyperparasitemia (>10% parasitized RBCs)
- Renal impairment (creatinine >265 μmol/L)
- Hyperlactatemia (lactate >5 mmol/L)

## P. vivax and P. ovale Malaria

### Blood-stage Treatment
- Chloroquine: 25 mg base/kg total over 3 days (10 mg/kg on days 1 and 2, 5 mg/kg on day 3)
- In chloroquine-resistant areas: ACT (same as for P. falciparum)

### Radical Cure (Anti-relapse)
- Primaquine: 0.25-0.5 mg/kg/day for 14 days (to eliminate hypnozoites)
- Tafenoquine: Single dose of 300 mg (adults; requires G6PD testing)
- G6PD testing is REQUIRED before prescribing primaquine or tafenoquine (risk of hemolysis)

## P. malariae and P. knowlesi Malaria
- Chloroquine: same dosing as P. vivax
- P. knowlesi can cause severe disease — treat as P. falciparum if severe

## Special Populations

### Pregnant Women
- First trimester: Quinine + clindamycin for 7 days (ACTs if this is not available)
- Second and third trimester: ACT (artemether-lumefantrine recommended)
- Severe malaria: IV artesunate at any stage of pregnancy

### Children Under 5 kg
- ACTs can be used; dose based on body weight
- Rectal artesunate for pre-referral treatment if injection not available

### Travelers (Non-immune)
- Treat promptly with ACT
- Higher risk of progression to severe disease
- Lower threshold for hospitalization

## Diagnosis

### Recommended Methods
- Microscopy (gold standard): Giemsa-stained thick and thin blood films
- Rapid Diagnostic Tests (RDTs): HRP2-based for P. falciparum; pLDH for all species
- All suspected cases should be parasitologically confirmed before treatment

### Parasitemia Classification
- Low: <1% of red blood cells parasitized (<50,000/μL)
- Moderate: 1-5% parasitized (50,000-250,000/μL)
- High/Severe: >5% parasitized (>250,000/μL) — medical emergency

## Prevention

### Chemoprophylaxis for Travelers
- Atovaquone-proguanil: Start 1-2 days before, daily during, 7 days after
- Doxycycline: Start 1-2 days before, daily during, 4 weeks after
- Mefloquine: Start 2-3 weeks before, weekly during, 4 weeks after

### Vector Control
- Long-lasting insecticidal nets (LLINs)
- Indoor residual spraying (IRS)
- Intermittent preventive treatment in pregnancy (IPTp) with sulfadoxine-pyrimethamine
- Seasonal malaria chemoprevention (SMC) in children in high-transmission areas
